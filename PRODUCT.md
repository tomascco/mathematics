# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Mathematics learners working through abstract algebra and related fields. They study alone, often late, returning to concepts multiple times. They need intuition before formalism and concrete examples before abstraction. Visual and interactive framing resonates: diagrams, computed tables, explorable parameters. The primary reader is Tomás, who drills into why definitions carry conditions, prefers side-by-side comparisons, and values failure cases that reveal structure.

## Product Purpose

A React component library and page framework for mathematical guides and interactive visualizations. Each guide is a long-form document covering a topic in depth: definitions, intuition boxes, theorems with proof sketches, worked examples, interactive explorers, and applications. Design is the product: the visual treatment of mathematical content is not decoration but pedagogy.

## Brand Personality

Methodical, warm, rigorous. A well-typeset mathematics monograph meeting a personal notebook. Confident enough to teach, human enough to admit where intuition outruns proof. Three words: **scholarly, structural, alive**.

## Anti-references

- **SaaS dashboards**: no rounded cards on gray backgrounds, no hero metrics, no admin-panel vocabulary
- **Wikipedia / textbook-dry**: no sterile reference layout with minimal visual identity, no walls of unstyled notation
- **Khan Academy / tutoring platforms**: no bubbly friendly UI, no gamification badges, no progress bars

## Design Principles

1. **Intuition before formalism.** Every definition opens with a concrete grasp, then tightens into precision. Layout reflects this: informal framing before the formal box.
2. **Distinct hierarchy for distinct mathematical objects.** Theorems, lemmas, propositions, definitions, corollaries, examples, and algorithms each deserve a visually distinct but typographically coherent treatment. A reader should recognize the object type before reading the label.
3. **Interactive where structure permits.** When a concept has parameters to vary, finite cases to enumerate, or diagrams to manipulate, provide an explorer. Interaction is pedagogy, not decoration.
4. **Progressive depth, not flat Encyclopedism.** A guide reads like a chapter, not a reference page. Sections build. Cross-references thread forward and back. The page has rhythm.
5. **Scholarly warmth, not academic sterility.** Ink on paper. Margins that breathe. Serifs that carry weight. Color used with the restraint of good marginalia, not the enthusiasm of a dashboard.
6. **Beautiful mathematical typesetting is non-negotiable.** Equations, symbols, and notation must render with the same typographic care as the prose around them. Display math should feel composed, not pasted in. Inline math must align to the x-height and baseline of surrounding text, not float above or below it. No pixelated bitmaps, no misaligned fractions, no cramped operators.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG 2.1 AA minimum
- Color is never the sole differentiator; mathematical objects use shape, border style, and label in addition to color
- Interactive elements require keyboard operation
- Reduced-motion preference respected for any scroll-triggered or expanding content
- Math content rendered as SVG or MathML with accessible text alternatives
